Who Owns America?

A brief look into what who owns businesses in America as collected by the United States Bureau of the Census.

Some may notice the large gap between business ownerships in America. Some may say that this gap is at record lows and will continue to decrease. That minorities own a large portion or the firms in America and that the gender gap is mostly apparent in the gender pay gap and business ownership does not apply. In 2012, just a few years ago, this did not hold true. According to the United States Bureau of the Census, there is a large gap between the percentage of men, women, minorities and non-minorities who own businesses in the United States. In 2012 the United States had nearly 28 million companies or firms. Surveys showed that 54% were owned primarily by men, 36% by women, 29% by minorities, and 69% by non minorities. These large gaps in who owns American businesses can bring up the question of who owns and runs America.

Total Firms: Represents the percentage of population per state that own their own business, that being 51% or more of the business. A strong trend can be seen where males consistently own the majority of the firms in each state. Nationally, this trend holds true.
Firms Owned by Men: Represents the percentage of male identifying population per state that own their own business.
Firms Owned by Women: Represents the percentage of female identifying population per state that own their own business. This percentage can consistently be seen coming up much lower than male business ownership.
Firms Owned by Minorities: Represents the percentage of minority population per state that owns their own business. Minority, as defined by the United States Bureau of the Census survey, constitutes as identifying withHispanics, Blacks or African Americans, American Indians and Alaska Natives, Asians, Native Hawaiians and Other Pacific Islanders, and/or owners of some other race not classified as "White non-Hispanic" owning 51 percent or more of the equity, interest, or stock of the business.
Firms not Owned by Non-minorities: Represents the percentage of non-minority population per state that owns their own business. As surveyed by the United States Bureau of the Census, this category is defined by Non-Hispanic Whites owning 51 percent or more of the equity, interest, or stock of the business.

In this data set, are all nonfarm businesses filing Internal Revenue Service tax forms as individual proprietorships, partnerships, or any type of corporation that have paid employees or no paid employees, and with receipts of $1,000 or more. An owner is defined by possessing 51% or more of the firm. Percentages may not add up to 100% as some owners had the option of selecting more than one race as well as Hispanic firms had the option to be of any race. Also, the owner had the option to select an unidentified gender or equally male/female.
Not included in the totals for any of these percentages are publicly held firms, foreign-owned companies, and not-for-profit companies.
Data: United States Bureau of the Census and Natural Earth
